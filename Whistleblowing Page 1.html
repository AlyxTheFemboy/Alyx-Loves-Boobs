Whistleblowing

Whistleblowing is the practice of reporting a witnessed or personally experienced case of malpractice in the workplace, usually with the intent of having the ne'erdowell punished for their actions.

It is commonly often also referenced to as "making a disclosure" or "blowing the whistle" (the second one being a bit more on the obvious side though).

Protected Acts of Whistleblowing Are:

Criminal Offenses

Health and Safety Mishaps

Risk of or Actual Environmental Damage

Miscarriage of Justice

Company-Wide Law Breaking (Incorrect Insurance, Tax Evasion, etc.)

Cover-Ups Of Wrongdoings



Acts Which Are Not Protected:

Personal Grievances (Unless They Are In Public Interest)
